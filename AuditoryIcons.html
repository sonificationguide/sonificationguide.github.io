Auditory Icons


Auditory Icons are the most commonly found sonification techniques. Auditory icons imitate sounds from the real world to help the listener to infer information such as the sound of crunching paper when a user empties their trash on a computer. Earcons are similar but more abstract and do not resemble a sound-information relationship. Often, musical motifs are used and “high levels of recognition can be achieved by careful use of pitch, rhythm and timbre” \cite{Earcons}. To distinguish earcons from one another, clear differences between the sounds are required such as tonal, timbral, and temporal changes.
